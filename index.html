A place for clear writing and kind design

We design for people who read and write: calm pages, generous spacing, and typographic choices that feel warm and thoughtful. Humanist Modernism is editorial, humane, and quietly practical.

Calm, human-centered pages designed for focused reading.

Design that listens to the reader — breathing room, clarity, and a human voice.

Stories, shaped for reading

We care about long-form clarity. That means a single column, a comfortable measure, and a rhythm that helps ideas arrive softly. Every choice — from line-height to tone — supports the experience of reading.

Comfortable reading

Layouts that keep line-length, contrast, and rhythm gentle on the eyes. Every element is designed for comfort and clarity, making long-form reading a pleasure.

Empathetic tone

Microcopy that sounds like a helpful human, not a machine. Every word is chosen to guide, reassure, and welcome the reader.

Accessible by default

Clear structure, keyboard-friendly focus states, and high contrast. Designed so everyone can read and interact with ease.

Design principles

Readable first. Make the text easy to follow. Kind microcopy. Tell people what will happen. Accessible by default. Semantic HTML, clear contrast, and touch-friendly controls.

Inspiration

We look to people-first typographers and clarity-minded designers: Frutiger’s calm practice, Tufte’s restraint, iA Writer’s typographic focus, and the careful systems built by contemporary type designers. These influences inform a gentle, editorial approach.
