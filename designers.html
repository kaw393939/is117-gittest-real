Designers

Stories of people whose work made reading clearer and design kinder.

Adrian Frutiger

Frutiger taught us that letters should be legible at a glance and comfortable across contexts. His calm, practical solutions remind designers that clarity comes from subtle, considered choices.

Erik Spiekermann

Erik bridged the craft of type with the needs of real systems. He shows how typographic care improves everyday reading — from signs to screens — by thinking about people first.

Matthew Carter

Matthew Carter’s work travels well between sizes and media. His letterforms give readers quiet guidance, helping words sit comfortably on the page and in the eye.

Edward Tufte

Tufte’s devotion to clarity and restraint is a touchstone: remove decoration that does not serve understanding, and let the content speak plainly.
