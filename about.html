About

Humanist Modernism is an editorial approach to design: it centers reading, calmness, and a voice that welcomes the reader. We favor typographic choices that make ideas easy to follow and microcopy that treats people kindly.

Philosophy

Good design makes reading effortless. That means a single column, generous margins, a comfortable measure, and type that breathes. We choose humanist sans for body copy and a warm serif for headlines to create an inviting, readable hierarchy. The goal is clarity without austerity.

Design that reduces friction is design that respects the reader’s time and attention.

Influences

Our work draws from a lineage of human-centered typographers and information designers.

  • Adrian Frutiger — clarity and legibility across sizes and contexts.
  • Edward Tufte — restraint and respect for data and content.
  • iA Writer — typographic focus, whitespace, and calm writing tools.
  • Contemporary accessibility practice — designing inclusively by default.

Human-Centered Microcopy

Write as you would speak to someone you care about. Be clear about outcomes, provide gentle guidance, and prefer short, tangible labels.

  • Use outcomes: "Save changes" rather than vague verbs.
  • Offer context: "Estimated reading time: 3 minutes."
  • Be helpful: "Need a hand? Open the quick guide."

Accessibility

Accessibility is not an add-on — it is the baseline. We ensure semantic HTML, clear headings, adequate contrast, and large tap targets. Typographic scale and spacing are chosen to make reading comfortable for the widest audience.
